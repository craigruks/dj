Biography

As a kid in Maine, sitting in the backseat of a boxy Volvo, I learned to pitch-match my vocals so well my mom couldn't hear it over the radio static. That's how I DJ now: transitions so seamless you'll feel them in your ribs before your ears catch on.

I don't blend in because I'm afraid to stand out. I blend in because calibration is more powerful than disruption. I match your energy, then elevate it. Basements, pools, rooftops, doesn't matter: I find the crowd's frequency, then modulate it.

Solo sets are Tetris: stacking beats that click into place. B2B is Pong-volleying rhythms until the crowd becomes the pixelated ball, dance moves ricocheting across the club.

Co-founder of Daddy Issue-crafting parties where you least expect them.
